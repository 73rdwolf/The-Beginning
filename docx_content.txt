SECTION 01
Overview
Reframe Visual is a B2B platform for eCommerce brands, photographers, and marketing agencies that delivers catalog-ready images through expert in-house human retouchers supported by an AI-powered workflow system. Clients select editing services, upload images, track progress in real time, review before paying, and are backed by a 100% refund guarantee if unsatisfied.

Core Value Proposition

Service Model — Critical Clarification
Humans do ALL image editing. AI is used exclusively for workflow operations — job routing, QA flagging, progress tracking, notifications, billing, and file management. AI does not touch, edit, or modify any customer image.

SECTION 02
Problem Statement
eCommerce brands, photographers, and agencies waste significant time and money on inconsistent, slow image editing workflows. The industry standard was built for volume resellers — not the businesses doing the selling.

Core Problems
Flat pricing punishes brands that only need basic edits — simple and complex cost the same
No real-time visibility into job progress — jobs disappear into a black box
Payment required upfront regardless of output quality
Revision cycles are slow, email-based, and imprecise — editors guess what the client means
Inconsistent output quality when work is outsourced to freelancers
Agencies charge per image whether the job takes 10 seconds or 10 minutes

SECTION 07
User Flows
7.1 Job Creation — 3-Step Self-Serve Flow
Step 1 — Service Selection  (/jobs/new/services)
User browses 19 services grouped by category: Core, Retouching, Specialized
Each service shows price, turnaround, and expandable before/after example
User can select a pre-built bundle OR build a custom service combination
Selecting compatible services triggers auto-suggestion for matching bundle
Must select at least 1 service before proceeding
Sticky bottom bar shows selected service count + estimated per-image price
If selection matches a bundle — system suggests switching and shows savings

Step 2 — Upload & Customization  (/jobs/new/upload)
Job name — free text (e.g. Spring Collection 2026)
Upload method — user chooses:
Direct upload: drag-and-drop or click to browse
Cloud: connect Google Drive or Dropbox via OAuth
Link: paste any shareable URL (WeTransfer, OneDrive, FTP, etc.)
Accepted formats: JPG, PNG, TIFF — max 50MB per image — max 100 images per job
All images in one job receive the same services — different service sets require separate jobs
Per-service settings panel — options vary by service type
Special instructions — free text (optional)
Reference images — upload to show preferred style (optional)
Complexity selection: Simple / Medium / Complex
Turnaround selection: 24h / 48h / 72h
Estimated delivery calculated dynamically from submission time
Sticky bottom bar shows image count, price calculation, and estimated delivery

Step 3 — Review & Submit  (/jobs/new/review)
Full order summary: job name, image count, service line items, total, delivery date
Each service line: service name + complexity + turnaround + unit price x quantity = subtotal
1 free revision per image per service — shown on summary
First order 20% discount applied automatically if applicable — shown in breakdown
CTA: 'Submit Job — Pay After Review' — no payment captured at submission

7.2 Complexity Assessment — Two-Tier Verification
Customer self-selects complexity during job creation (Simple / Medium / Complex)
After upload, in-house editors review uploaded images
If complexity differs from customer selection:
System generates updated price
Email sent to customer with: original vs revised complexity, reason, new total, approve/decline link
Customer must approve before work begins
If customer disputes — manual review available
Note: Email subject: 'Action Required: Price Update for Job #[ID]'

7.3 Job Tracking  (/jobs/[id])
Overall progress bar with percentage and ETA
Service-level progress: Complete (green) / In Progress (blue) / Pending (gray)
Activity feed — timestamped log of job events
Editor assignment visible — in-house editor name/initial + senior QA reviewer
Page updates within 60 seconds of any pipeline state change

7.4 Image Review & Approval  (/jobs/[id]/review)
Side-by-side before/after viewer with zoom and pan
Stage navigation: Original → each service applied → Final
Per-image approval or revision request
Annotation tools for revision requests: circles, arrows, freehand draw, text pins, color-coded markers
Bulk action: Approve All — approves all remaining images at once
Thumbnail strip — color coded: approved / revision / current / pending
Sticky review progress bar: approved count / revision count / pending count / running total
'Go to Payment' unlocks when all images have a decision (approved or revision submitted)
Previews are watermarked — high-res files unlock after payment

7.5 Revision Request Workflow
Triggered by 'Request Revision' on any image in the review interface
Customer selects which service needs revision (or multiple)
Customer uses annotation tools to mark the exact area on the image
Customer adds free-text description of the issue
Optional reference image upload
1 free revision included per image per service — counter shown in modal
Revisions delivered within 12–24 hours
⚠  Additional revisions beyond the 1 free — pricing TBD. Recommend $2/image. Confirm with founders.

7.6 Payment Flow  (/jobs/[id]/payment)
Customer submits job — no payment captured
Team reviews images and confirms or adjusts complexity pricing
Customer approves price (if changed) before editing begins
In-house editors complete the work
Customer reviews watermarked previews of edited images
Customer approves images or requests revisions
Once all images reviewed — customer proceeds to payment
Payment page shows approved images only — revision images excluded until approved
Customer pays via Stripe, PayPal, Bank Transfer, or Invoice (enterprise)
High-resolution downloads unlock immediately after payment
Files available for 30 days after payment

7.7 Refund Policy
100% refund if unsatisfied after revision has been delivered
Refund triggered via customer support request or self-serve in dashboard (confirm with founders)
No questions asked — full refund issued

Watermarked previews surface to customer in review interface
Customer reviews, annotates, approves or requests revisions
Revisions handled within 12–24h — customer notified on completion
Customer approves all images and proceeds to payment
